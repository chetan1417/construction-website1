The construction industry plays an important role in competitiveness and prosperity of the economy in terms of its significantly contributing to shelter, infrastructure and employment (Anaman and Osei-Amponsah, 2007). A number of researchers have investigated on the vital role of the construction sector to the overall economic growth. In Rhodes’ paper who stated the construction industry in the UK contributed £83.0 billion in economic output in 2012, which accounts for 6% of the total. Also, workforce jobs in the construction industry in UK were 2.03 million in 2012 and 2.12 million in 2013, account 6.4% and 6.5% in total respectively (C. Rhodes, 2013). Oladinrin indicated the significance of construction linkage with the aggregate economy in Nigeria by studying on construction output and Gross Domestic Project (Oladinrin and etc., 2012). A recent report of U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis showed that the GDP value added of construction industry was $652,723 million in 2014, 3.7% of total GDP (http://www.bea.gov/industry/gdpbyind_data.htm). Besides, a report of U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics stated the employment by construction industry sector take 4.7%, 3.9% and 4.5% in the year of 2002, 2012 and 2022 respectively (http://www.bls.gov/emp/ep_table_201.htm). Our Story

The Company Coro Construction Limited was established by Late.Sh.Bhagat Singh Thakur in 1890 in Shimla(British India). At that time it work under the East India Comapany and was known HCL "Himalayan Construction Limited" The contract of Kalka Shimla Railway Line was given to HCL in 1898 by East India Company. The railway was built under the direction of Construction between 1898 and 1903 to connect Shimla, the summer capital of India during the British Raj.After the 1947 when Indai got independence our Company Come into indentity and company name was changed to Coro Constructions. The contract of The Bhakra-Nangal Dam(Bilaspur,Himachal Pradesh) was the secoond biggest project on which our company worked upon. Construction of the dam started in 1948; Jawaharlal Nehru poured the first bucket of concrete into the dry riverbed of the Sutlej on 17 November 1955, as a symbolic initiation of the work. Addressing a gathering there, he said, "This is a gift to the people of India and to the future generations from the workers who built this dam", calling for "rais[ing] a memorial" at the dam "in honour" of the workers.The dam was completed by the end of 1963. Successive stages were completed by the early 1970s.After 1970s comapany the command and business of company was given to Son of Bhagat Singh Thakur, Sh.Gulab Sing Thakur.After 1985 our company got international appearance in the world.In between 1985 and 2000 we have worked upon more then five biggest projects.Now its 2022 and our company is growing more and more.
